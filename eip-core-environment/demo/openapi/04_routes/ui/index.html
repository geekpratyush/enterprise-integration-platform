ISO 20022 Knowledge

Explore high-fidelity MT to MX mapping guidelines and CBPR+ examples.

The transition from legacy SWIFT MT (Message Type) formats to the ISO 20022 MX (XML) standard is one of the most significant overhauls in global financial messaging. While MT and MX messages can often be translated back and forth, they are not strictly 1-to-1. MX messages are much larger and data-rich, meaning MX-to-MT (down-translation) often results in data truncation, whereas MT-to-MX (up-translation) usually requires filling mandatory MX fields with default values if the MT lacks the data.
