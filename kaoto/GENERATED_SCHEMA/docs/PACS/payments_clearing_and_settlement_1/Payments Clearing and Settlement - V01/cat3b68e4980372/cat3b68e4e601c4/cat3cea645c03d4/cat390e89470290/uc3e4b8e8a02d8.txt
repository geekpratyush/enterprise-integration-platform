Definition : the process of reconciliation is the process of matching one type of business data with another type of business data.

As reconciliation applies to several areas in the customer-to-bank, bank-to-customer and customer-to-customer domain, further subdefinitions are given below : 

a) Reconciliation of payment in transit (ie reconciliation from debtor's point of view)

Definition : the debtor closes the payment in transit upon receipt of the debit advice from the first or forwarding agent. 

As his administrative process will keep track of the payment submitted, the positive confirmation of execution (ie the confirmation of debit) received from the first or forwarding agent will allow the debtor to close the payment order in transit. If currency conversion has been applied by his first agent, he will also be able to determine exchange rate differences and book them properly.

Pre-condition : payment has been submitted and is waiting for confirmation of execution.

Trigger : receipt of  a debit-advice.

Post-condition : payment has been properly booked as being paid and closed as payment in transit.


b) Reconciliation of Open invoices (ie reconciliation from creditor's point of view)

Definition : based on the remittance details the creditor receives, the creditor will change the status of  open invoices to "paid" and book possible corrections applied on the invoice amounts. Total amount will be booked as Incoming payment in transit.

c) Reconciliation of bank-to-customer statement.

Definition : both the debtor and the creditor reconcile the payments as reported on the bank-to-customer statement with the payments in transit. If currency conversion has been applied the eventual exchange rate differences are booked. Charges are booked as well.

Pre-condition : payments are registered as in transit, waiting for final confirmation of posting to the account from the account-servicing agent.

Trigger : bank-to-customer statement of account received.

Post-condition : payment in transit matches with reported debit or credit on bank-to-customer statement and financial consequences are booked.




Pre-condition : the creditor waits for payment of his open invoices.

Trigger : receipt of the credit advice from the final agent or the cheque with attached remittance details or a separate remittance advice message. 

Post-condition : open invoices closed and total amount booked as incoming payment in transit.
