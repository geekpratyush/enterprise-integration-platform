In this scenario, the originating party (the one who receives the invoice from the creditor) is for example a subsidiary.  However, the subsidiary sends all of its invoices to Head Office, which owns the account at the first agent. Head Office is therefore the debtor. The debtor, however, is using a shared service centre to initiate payments to its bank.  The shared service centre is only identified as initiating party. The shared service will not need to be identified further down in the interbank chain.

Notes: 

1) Assumption is that Payment Initiation Status is positive. The sending of and the content of a Payment Initiation Status message depends on bilateral service agreed between first agent and initiating party.
2)  At requested execution date, the first agent will debit the account of the debtor.  OOnce the first agent debits the account of the debtor (at requested execution date), it may  need to generate customer debit advice and/or statement - depending on service agreed between debtor/initiating party and the first agent and depending on legal requirements. The advice and/or statement info will normally be sent to the debtor, unless otherwise agreed between debtor and first agent.
3) There is no indication of chronological order in the status/debit advice/statement information sent in the scenario above, as this will also depend on agreements between debtor/initiating party and the first agent, but banks implementing and offering these services will define a logical, chronological sequence for sending these messages.
4) Furthermore, there may also be interbank advices/statements exchanged, but these are not included in the diagram as this will depend on the settlement method chosen between first and final agent.
